# Phase 0 Customer Discovery — Interview Scripts

**Purpose:** Validate willingness to pay for a Sri Lanka property intelligence report before writing production code. Three audiences, three distinct scripts. Target volumes: **10 legal firms, 20 diaspora buyers, 5 bank relationship managers.**

**Format:** 25–35 minutes, video or phone. Ask, do not pitch. The goal of every call is to walk away with (a) a concrete workflow description, (b) a price anchor, and (c) a named next step.

**Ground rules:**
- Do not show a product demo in the first 15 minutes.
- Record (with consent) or take structured notes — fill in the template at the end of each script during or immediately after the call.
- If the interviewee volunteers a feature request, note it but do NOT promise it.
- End every call by asking for two referrals.

## Script 1 — Conveyancing Law Firms

**Target roles:** Partner, Senior Associate, or Conveyancing Manager at firms handling residential transfers (sale, gift, mortgage bond).
**Target firm shortlist:** D.L. & F. De Saram, Varners, F.J. & G. de Saram, Nithya Partners, Julius & Creasy, plus five boutique firms with diaspora clientele (identify via Colombo Bar Association or Google).
**Pilot price to test:** USD 50 single report / USD 200 five-pack / USD 350 ten-pack.

### Opening (2 min)
> "Thanks for making the time. I'm [Name], I run a data platform covering Sri Lankan property listings. I'm not here to sell you anything today — I'm trying to understand the due-diligence work that happens before a residential transfer, and where outside data would actually save you time. Twenty-five minutes, then I'll ask for two referrals and let you go. Is that OK?"

### Workflow (8 min)
1. When a client comes to you for a residential purchase, walk me through the first 48 hours — what do you do, what does your paralegal do?
2. Who in the firm checks the **market price** of the property the client is buying — is that even in scope for you, or does the client handle it separately?
3. How do you currently verify that the asking price is reasonable? (Listen for: "we don't"; "we ask a valuer friend"; "we look at LankaPropertyWeb"; "the client brings a chartered valuer report".)
4. How long does a standard due-diligence pack take you to assemble end-to-end? Which parts are slow?
5. What goes wrong most often — what's the typical issue that delays a closing?

### Pain intensity (6 min)
6. In the last twelve months, has a deal fallen through or gone sour because the price turned out to be off-market or the property had a hidden risk (flood, title, zoning)? Tell me about it.
7. If your paralegal could produce a 6-page "property intelligence pack" in 30 seconds for any listing URL — asking-price context, recent comparables, flood risk, days-on-market — would you use it? What would you need to trust it?
8. Who would be the internal decision-maker on whether your firm subscribed to a tool like that — you, managing partner, COO?

### Pricing anchor (5 min)
9. Today, when a client asks you "is the price fair", do you charge for that check? How is it billed — hourly, flat, included?
10. **If** such a report existed and took two paralegal-hours out of your workflow, what would you pay per report? [Listen silently. Do not volunteer a number.]
11. Follow-up if they name a number below USD 30: "Interesting — what's anchoring you to that figure?"
12. Follow-up if they name a number above USD 70: "Would your firm pay that, or would you pass it through to the client as a disbursement?"
13. Which billing model fits you better — pay-per-report, a monthly retainer covering N reports, or a white-labelled report with your logo at a markup to the client?

### Commitment (4 min)
14. If I showed you a sample report next week on one of your current deals — free — would you be willing to give me an honest critique of it?
15. If the report turns out to be useful and priced right, how many deals per month would you realistically use it on?
16. Anyone else in your firm I should speak to? Any peer firm you'd recommend I talk to?

### Close (1 min)
> "This has been genuinely useful. I'll send a one-paragraph email summary of what I heard so you can correct me if I've misunderstood anything. Thanks."

## Script 2 — Diaspora Buyers

**Target:** Sri Lankan-origin individuals living in UK, Australia, US, Canada, Middle East who have bought OR are seriously considering buying property in Sri Lanka in the last 24 months.
**Find them via:** Sri Lankan community Facebook groups, LinkedIn diaspora groups, alumni groups (Royal, Thurstan, Ananda, Visakha, etc.), WhatsApp referrals from Script 1 lawyers.
**Pilot price to test:** USD 79 single / USD 299 five-pack, plus free "snapshot" lead magnet.

### Opening (2 min)
> "Hi, thanks for the time. I'm building a service for overseas Sri Lankans buying property back home. I'm not selling anything today — I'm trying to learn what the experience was actually like for you. About 25 minutes. That OK?"

### Journey (10 min)
1. Tell me about the property search — when did you start, what were you looking for, where?
2. How did you find listings from overseas? What sources did you trust, what sources did you not trust?
3. Did you travel back to see properties, or did someone on the ground view on your behalf? Who?
4. What was the scariest part of the process? (Listen for: price fairness; title fraud; family-agent conflicts of interest; hidden property defects; flood / landslide risk; delays.)
5. Did anyone in your network get burned on a Sri Lankan property deal? What happened?

### Verification pain (6 min)
6. How did you satisfy yourself that the asking price was fair? Who did you trust to tell you that?
7. Did you commission a chartered valuer's report? How did you pick them? What did it cost, how long did it take?
8. In an ideal world, before you committed any money, what would you have wanted to know about the property that you couldn't easily find out from overseas?

### Product reaction (5 min)
9. If I described a 6-page independent English-language PDF report that covered the asking-price context, comparable recent listings, flood/landslide risk, distance to schools and hospitals, and time-on-market — all keyed off a listing URL you pasted in — would you have used that?
10. Would you pay for that, or would you expect your cousin / agent / lawyer to do it for you as part of the deal?
11. What price feels right to you? [Silence.]
12. Would you want a free preview — the first three sections — and pay to unlock the full report, or would you pay for the full thing up front?

### Commitment (3 min)
13. Are you still actively looking, or have you closed a deal?
14. Would you give me your feedback on a sample report on a property you're considering or have recently bought? Free, no strings.
15. Anyone else you know who's in the middle of buying from overseas I should talk to?

### Close (1 min)
> "Really appreciate this. I'll drop you a link to a short summary once I've finished the interviews. Thanks."

## Script 3 — Bank Relationship Managers

**Target roles:** Head of Mortgage Products, Head of Retail Credit, Head of Collateral Valuation, or equivalent at HDFC Bank, Sampath, NDB, DFCC, Commercial Bank.
**Explicitly excluded in Phase 0:** HNB (existing LPW partnership — conflict).
**Price to anchor:** USD 1,000–2,000 / month pilot; bulk AVM USD 0.50–2 per address for portfolio monitoring. Treat Phase 0 as discovery only — do NOT quote firm pricing.

### Opening (2 min)
> "Thanks for your time. I'm researching how Sri Lankan banks handle mortgage-collateral valuation — where it's slow, where it's expensive, where an automated tool would fit. I'm not pitching anything specific today. Thirty minutes?"

### Current state (10 min)
1. Walk me through the end-to-end mortgage approval — from application to drawdown. Where does the collateral valuation sit in that flow?
2. Do you use a panel of external chartered valuers, an in-house valuation team, or a mix? How large is the panel?
3. Per mortgage application, what's the typical valuation turnaround and typical cost — the one you pay the valuer, not what you charge the customer?
4. After drawdown, do you revalue collateral periodically? At what cadence — annually, on default trigger, never?
5. For your existing mortgage book, when was the last time the whole portfolio's collateral value was refreshed, and how was it done?

### Pain (6 min)
6. What goes wrong with valuations today? (Listen for: turnaround delays holding up approvals; valuer disputes; over-valuation leading to LTV breaches at foreclosure; under-valuation losing deals to competitors.)
7. Has the bank ever had a loss event where a valuation turned out to be materially wrong? What did you change afterwards?
8. How do you handle portfolio-level risk monitoring — do you track collateral value at portfolio level, or only per loan?

### Product fit (8 min)
9. If you had an API that returned a price estimate — with a confidence range and a methodology statement — in under two seconds for any residential address, where in your workflow would it first appear? (Listen for: pre-screening at application; parallel to chartered valuer; post-default checks; portfolio monitoring.)
10. What would the AVM have to look like for your risk function or CBSL supervisor to be comfortable with it sitting in the workflow? (Listen for: methodology disclosure; MdAPE; calibration to actual sold prices; E&O cover; regulatory approval status.)
11. Is there a specific product line — first-home loans, green-field condos, plot-only lending — where a fast AVM would help more than others?
12. If this existed and was CBSL-acceptable, what's the fit — would it replace an in-person valuation, or sit alongside as a screening tool?
13. How do procurement and IT onboarding work at your bank for a product like this? Who owns the decision — Credit, Risk, IT, or Procurement?

### Commitment (3 min)
14. If I were to put together a one-page concept note and a sample AVM output on 50 properties from your region, who inside the bank would be the right person to review it with me?
15. What would make you say "no" to a pilot — what are the dealbreakers?

### Close (1 min)
> "This is exactly the ground-truth I needed. I'll send a short summary of what I heard; please correct me if I've got anything wrong. Thank you."

## Synthesis at the end of Phase 0

After all 35 interviews, write `docs/phase0_discovery.md` with:
1. **Demand matrix** — count of "I would pay" by audience at each price anchor.
2. **Pricing findings** — median / range of volunteered price anchors.
3. **Top three product requirements** surfaced in each audience.
4. **Warm intro list** — people willing to see a sample report.
5. **Decision vs plan** — does the Phase 5a USD 50 price hold? Any pricing changes?

**Go/no-go gate:** proceed to Phase 1 code work only if ≥3 of 10 legal firms explicitly said they would pay the pilot price.
